Assignment 3: Robotic Arm Project

Introduction Design Process Parts Assembly Challenges Animation

Building a Robotic Arm Using Fusion 360

The objective of this project was to design, assemble, and animate a robotic arm with a functional gripper using Fusion 360. The robotic arm was modeled part by part, assembled carefully, and then animated to demonstrate its working mechanism. Throughout this process, various challenges were encountered and resolved to achieve the final output.

Design and Assembly Process

Individual Part Modeling

Each component of the robotic arm, including the base, joints, links, and gripper, was designed separately in Fusion 360 to ensure proper fit and functionality.

Assembly of Components

  • All the designed parts were assembled in Fusion 360.
  • Constraints and joints were applied to enable movement in a controlled manner.
  • Several issues, such as dimension errors and misalignment, were identified and corrected.

Robotic Arm Parts

Base

The foundation that provides stability to the entire arm structure.

Arm Link 1

The primary connecting segment that attaches to the base.

Arm Link 2

The secondary connecting segment that extends the arm's reach.

Joint

Connects different arm segments and enables rotational movement.

Gripper

The end effector designed to grasp and manipulate objects.

Assembly Design

The assembly process involved carefully connecting all individual parts while ensuring proper alignment and functionality:

Step 1: Base Setup

The base was positioned and fixed as the foundation for the entire assembly.

Step 2: Primary Joint Connection

The first joint was connected to the base with appropriate constraints to allow rotational movement.

Step 3: Arm Links Assembly

The arm links were connected sequentially with joints in between to create the main structure.

Step 4: Gripper Installation

The gripper mechanism was attached to the end of the arm with proper constraints for opening and closing movements.

Challenges Faced

Dimension Errors

  • Some components did not fit correctly due to miscalculations in dimensions.
  • Adjustments were made to ensure accurate fitting.

Positioning Errors

  • Parts were initially placed incorrectly, affecting movement.
  • Correct alignment and constraints were applied to resolve this.

Animation Issues

  • Initially, individual parts moved independently instead of a synchronized motion.
  • Research was conducted to understand animation techniques in Fusion 360.
  • Proper constraints and motion sequences were applied to achieve realistic movement.

Animation

The animation demonstrates the full range of motion and functionality of the robotic arm:

Animation Process

  1. Setting up joint constraints to define possible movements
  2. Creating keyframes for different positions of the arm
  3. Defining the motion path and timing between keyframes
  4. Adding smooth transitions between movements
  5. Rendering the final animation to showcase the arm's functionality

Final Outcome

  • A fully assembled robotic arm with a functional gripper.
  • A working animation demonstrating its movement and functionality.
  • A better understanding of Fusion 360 modeling, assembly constraints, and animation techniques.

Conclusion

This project provided hands-on experience in designing, assembling, and animating a robotic arm using Fusion 360. Overcoming various challenges enhanced problem-solving skills and deepened the understanding of mechanical design principles. The final animation successfully showcased the working mechanism of the robotic arm.
